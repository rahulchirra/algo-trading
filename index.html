Understanding Financial Markets

Financial markets are platforms where buyers and sellers come together to trade financial instruments like stocks, bonds, currencies, and derivatives. They facilitate the flow of capital between investors and those who need funds.

Financial Market Ecosystem

Capital Allocation

Money flows from investors to businesses that need it to grow

Price Discovery

Determines fair value of assets through supply and demand

Liquidity

Allows quick conversion of investments into cash

Risk Management

Provides tools to protect against financial risks

Advantages

  • Enable economic growth by connecting capital with productive uses
  • Provide investment opportunities for individuals to grow wealth
  • Offer liquidity, allowing quick conversion of assets to cash

Limitations/Risks

  • Market volatility can lead to significant losses
  • Requires knowledge and expertise to navigate effectively
  • Subject to manipulation and fraud in some cases

Types of Financial Markets

Stock Markets

Markets where shares of publicly traded companies are bought and sold.

Example:

Buying 10 shares of Apple at $150 each gives you ownership in Apple Inc. If the stock rises to $170, your investment grows to $1,700.

Ownership in companies

Forex Markets

The global marketplace for trading national currencies against one another.

Example:

An American company selling in Japan converts Yen to Dollars to pay employees through the forex market.

Currency exchange

Cryptocurrency

Digital marketplaces where cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin are traded 24/7.

Example:

Buying 0.1 Bitcoin at $30,000. If Bitcoin rises to $40,000, your investment grows to $4,000.

Blockchain-based

Derivatives

Markets for instruments whose value derives from underlying assets.

Example:

A wheat farmer sells futures contracts at $5/bushel to guarantee price, protecting against potential price drops.

Order Types, Market Participants, and Trading Mechanics

Order Types Explained

Market Order

Buy/sell immediately at best available price

Example: "Buy 100 shares of Microsoft now at current price"

Limit Order

Buy/sell only at specified price or better

Example: "Buy Tesla only if price drops to $200 or lower"

Stop Order

Becomes market order once specified price is reached

Example: "If Apple falls to $140, sell immediately to limit losses"

Stop-Limit Order

Triggers at stop price but executes only at limit price or better

Example: "If Tesla hits $250, sell only if I can get at least $248"
